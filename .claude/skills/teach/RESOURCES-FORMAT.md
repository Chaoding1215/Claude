# RESOURCES.md Format

Curate trusted sources on the topic. Distinguish between two categories:

## Knowledge

Educational materials like textbooks and evidence-based articles, each with a single-line annotation explaining coverage and use cases.

## Wisdom

Communities—both online and local—offering practical feedback and real-time guidance, similarly annotated.

## Key Guidelines

- Prioritize quality over quantity; sources must meet high-trust standards: primary materials, recognized authorities, peer-reviewed research, and well-moderated communities.
- Exclude marketing disguised as education.
- Every entry demands a brief explanatory note. Bare URLs lose utility quickly.
- Document gaps—areas where reliable resources don't yet exist—to guide future research priorities.
- Prune incorrect, shallow, or off-mission sources rather than burying them.
- Record user preferences, such as opting out of community participation, to prevent redundant suggestions in future sessions.
