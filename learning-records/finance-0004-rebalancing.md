## What Was Covered

- Portfolio drift: at 10% stocks / 4% bonds, a 60/40 becomes ~72/28 after 10 years without rebalancing
- Interactive drift simulator: year slider → shows drifted stock % and the extra crash loss vs rebalanced
- Two strategies:
  - Calendar rebalancing: simple, predictable; more transactions
  - Threshold rebalancing (±5%): fewer transactions, more tax-efficient
- Rebalancing band visualization: green zone 55–65%, marker moves with drift
- The hidden gift: rebalancing is systematic buy-low-sell-high without prediction
- Tax optimization: rebalance in tax-advantaged accounts first; use new contributions to rebalance before selling
- 4 smart tactics: new contributions, dividend redirection, tax-advantaged accounts first, tolerate ±5–10% drift

## Non-Obvious Insights to Preserve

- Rebalancing is primarily risk management, not return enhancement. In trending markets (e.g., US stocks 2010–2020), not rebalancing would have given higher absolute returns. The benefit is keeping risk aligned with the investor's capacity — not chasing yield.
- The "rebalancing bonus" (~0.5% p.a.) cited in some literature only materializes in mean-reverting volatile markets. It does not reliably appear in all regimes.
- Using new contributions to rebalance (rather than selling) can eliminate capital gains taxes entirely — the most underrated tactic for most personal investors.
