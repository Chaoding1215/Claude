## What Was Covered

- Behavior gap: Morningstar data shows investors earn ~1.7% p.a. less than the funds they hold
- Compounding illustration: on 1M CNY over 20 years, behavior gap costs ~1.31M
- Six core biases: loss aversion, recency bias, disposition effect, overconfidence, anchoring, herding
- Emotion-market cycle map: peak buying at euphoria, peak selling at despair
- Four systematic fixes: Investment Policy Statement (IPS), automation/DCA, reduce info noise, threshold rebalancing rules
- Core principle: system > willpower — design rules in calm markets, execute mechanically in chaos

## Non-Obvious Insights to Preserve

- The behavior gap is caused by *capital-weighted* vs *time-weighted* return measurement difference — investors have more money in at tops and less at bottoms, so they experience proportionally more of the losses.
- Loss aversion (~2x asymmetry) is a feature, not a bug, of the human brain — it evolved for survival contexts where losses (death) were unrecoverable. It misfires in investment contexts.
- "Controlling emotions" is the wrong goal. The right goal is designing a system where your emotions don't get a vote at the critical moments.
