## What Was Covered

**Lesson**: `finance-0001-why-allocation-beats-selection.html`

Core insight introduced: asset allocation accounts for ~90% of long-term portfolio return variability (Brinson, Hood & Beebower, 1991). The hierarchy of investment decisions:

1. Asset allocation (dominant factor)
2. Security/fund selection (limited marginal impact)
3. Market timing (empirically ineffective)
4. Cost control (hidden but compounding impact)

Key concepts introduced:
- Six major asset classes: emerging market equities, developed market equities, REITs, commodities, bonds, cash
- Risk-return relationship: higher expected return = higher short-term volatility, always
- Time horizon as the primary constraint on equity exposure
- Liquidity needs override theoretical risk tolerance

## Non-Obvious Insights to Preserve

- The Brinson study is often misquoted as "asset allocation determines 90% of returns." More precisely: it determines 90% of the *variance* (volatility) of returns across portfolios. The distinction matters — stock picking still affects absolute returns, just not the variance across portfolios.
- "Risk tolerance" is psychological; "risk capacity" is structural (liquidity, time horizon). Confusing the two is a common and costly mistake.
