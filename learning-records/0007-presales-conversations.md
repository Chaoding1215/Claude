# LR-0007: Presales Conversation Patterns

**Status**: active
**Lesson**: `0009-presales-conversations.html`

## What Was Covered

- Four buyer personas: CISO (risk/strategic), Security Architect (technical/integration), IT Manager (operational), CFO (TCO/ROI)
- Five discovery questions with diagnostic annotations — reveals SOC maturity, real pain, budget trigger, priorities, reporting structure
- 80/20 discovery rule: customer talks 80%, you talk 20%
- Six common objections with response patterns: incumbent vendor, "not a priority," price, "we're too small," build internally, prior bad experience
- Competitive positioning across three scenarios: vs. pure-play product vendor, vs. competing MDR/MSSP, vs. "we'll do it ourselves"
- The presales conversation arc: Qualify → Discover → Position → Demonstrate → Advance
- Core principle: every step earns the right to the next one

## Key Frameworks to Remember

- **Build vs. buy math**: Internal SOC = 6–8 FTEs × $100–150K loaded = $600K–$1.2M/year before tooling. Use this number.
- **TCO reframe**: Never compare license prices — compare license + management burden + people cost + incident risk.
- **Discovery before demo**: pitching without discovery = guessing. Discovery earns the right to the demo.
- **Bridge offer**: when customer wants to build internally, propose co-managed model that covers them during the build.
